As a man in his 20's searching for the person he wants to be, I often reflect on the experiences that created the person I am today. 

Today, I am a software engineer who works closely with GenAI[^GenAI]. I enjoy what I do; yet, in my personal life, I find myself becoming progressively more disillusioned with the state of Tech. For most of human history, innovations helped make the average person's life better. Now, it feels like the most prevalent technologies either distract us from what truly matters or are created solely as a vehicle for profit. Suffice to say, this was not always my viewpoint. In fact, in my youth, much of my happiness came from digital technology.

### The Box Opens

When I was twelve, I discovered how to jailbreak[^jailbreak] my iPhone 4. That very night, instead of sleeping, I spent hours under my blanket tinkering with the design of the home screen. My child self was overwhelmed by the seemingly infinite possibilities at my fingertips. The following morning, my poor decision caught up to me as I struggled to keep my eyes open in school. Unsurprisingly, this feeble consequence did not deter me from pursuing my new obsession. Whether it be on the car ride home, at church, or wherever I found myself to be, I would spend all my time browsing Cydia[^cydia], looking for cool packages to try. Unbeknownst to little Anjie, the honeymoon period was soon to end. 

Around that time, newer iPhones came with this voice assistant, "Siri", that replaced the Voice Controls interface that their predecessors had. Thinking she was really dope, I looked for packages that could add her, and ultimately tried installing one.

...

**It bricked my phone.**

Whoever published that package probably had a good chuckle at the expense of a child who had *barely* seen his thirteenth summer. After several fruitless attempts at salvaging my phone, I reluctantly accepted the futility. In the end, I factory reset my phone... and promptly went back to tinkering. The experience, albeit discouraging, was one of my first true tastes of technological curiosity. 

And I loved it.

Jailbreaking my iPhone, downloading Pokemon on my calculator, pursuing a Computer Science degree. At first, I was purely a consumer, but making them my own offered an entirely new satisfaction. I suspect, for little me, it provided a place where I felt like I was in control[^author-note-1], something that was sparse in the rest of my life. 

Those initial sparks of sincere curiosity ignited the nurturing flame that gave birth to my career in the tech industry.

### Sickness, Death, Misery, and the Lot

It is without a doubt that technology has defined my life up until now. Even after all this time, there is still that kid inside me who is fascinated when seeing what the latest LLMs[^llms] are capable of. Yet, silently festering within me is a less enthusiastic voice. 

#### On Novelty

I no longer get the same fulfillment from new things. The child that seeked novelty is qwelled by the adult that seeks stability. When I was exploring and learning the world, everything new was so pure and exciting. Now, as an arrogant young man, everything new challenges my world view. When I look back, perhaps with the rose-tinted glasses that was my childlike naivete, it felt like every incremental release pushed the boundaries of human capabilities. Now that Tech is the biggest industry, every idea feels so soulless — no longer pushing the boundaries of human achievement, but numbers on a stock exchange.

#### On Social Media

Social media apps are some of the most valuable products of all time. Yet, they are "free". They operate without any monetary cost to the user because of how valuable our collection attention is. And, if our attention is so valuable, shouldn't we be more intentional on what we choose to invest it in?

#### On Artificial Intelligence

Occasionally, I will speak to someone who is terrified of what AI could do to their livelihood. I feel the same way, yet I have difficulty looking into their eyes. Why do we have to be more productive? Why does number need to go up? Yes, people who do not adapt will be left behind. This has always been the case. But *why* does that have to be the case? Is our purpose on this Earth to make our own lives better at the expense of others? 

Who will speak for me when *I'm* the one left behind?

### But Elpis, Alone, Remained Inside

Ultimately, my souring relationship with technology may also just be a microcosm of my growing distrust in institutions. Seeing genocides, invasions, and unrelentless greed over and over again in the short 20-or-so years I have been on this Earth has slowly eroded my belief in human good. 

Even so, I hope that my cynical view of modern technology is unfounded. For, as a nobody who still has attachments to this world, what else can I do, but hope?
