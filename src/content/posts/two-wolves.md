### Pandora's Box

When I was twelve, I discovered how to jailbreak[^jailbreak] my iPhone 4. That very night, instead of sleeping, I spent hours under my blanket tinkering with the user interface. My child self was captivated by the seemingly endless possibilities. The following school day, I could hardly keep my eyes open in class; this feeble consequence did not deter me from my new obsession. Whether it be on the car ride home, at church, or wherever I found myself to be, I would spend my time browsing Cydia[^cydia], looking for cool packages to try. 

Unfortunately, the honeymoon period was soon to end. I had thought for a while that the "Siri" assistant that came with the newer iPhone models was really dope; so, one day, I tried downloading a package that claimed to add it.

...

**It bricked my phone.**

I felt slighted. Whoever published that package probably had a good chuckle at the expense of a child who had *barely* seen his thirteenth summer. After reconciling with the fact that there was nothing to be done, I factory reset the phone and went right back to tinkering. Albeit discouraging, I had one of my first true tastes of technological curiosity.

Jailbreaking my iPhone, downloading Pokemon on my calculator, building my own computer. In my adolescence, I was fascinated with every piece technology. Learning the ins and outs of a device offered a level of satisfaction that was rarely found elsewhere. I suspect, for little me, it provided a space where I felt like *I* was in control, a place that *I* understood[^author-note-1]. A place... that was just for me. 

Those initial sparks of curiosity ignited the nurturing flame that gave birth to my career in the tech industry.

---

Up until now, it is without a doubt that technology has defined my life. As someone who works in the GenAI[^GenAI] space, I am constantly aware of frontier developments in the most topical technology of our time, Artifical Intelligence. Even after all this time, there is still that kid inside me who is fascinated when seeing what the newest models are capable of. However, in recent years, there has been a less enthusiastic voice in my head, silently festering. 

I no longer get the same fulfillment from new technologies. The child that seeked novelty has been qwelled by the adult that seeks stability. When I was exploring and learning the world, everything new was so pure and exciting. Now, as an arrogant young adult who thinks he has a concrete idea of how the world should function, everything new challenges my world view.

When I look back, perhaps with the rose-tinted glasses that was my childlike naivete, it felt like every incremental release pushed the boundaries of human innovation. Now that Tech is the biggest industry, every new idea feels so soulless, like a cog in the capitalist machine. No longer pushing the boundaries of human achievement, but numbers on a stock exchange.

When I speak to people who are so scared of what AI could do to their livelihood, I sometimes have difficulty looking into their eyes. Yes, people who do not adapt can sometimes be left behind. But why does that have to be the case? Who will speak for me when I'm the one left behind?


How will I navigate these contradictions?
